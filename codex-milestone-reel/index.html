Gustavo's operating shift

The real unlock is working on the flow of the work.

What feels most powerful is not just that Codex writes files. It is that Gustavo can stay in the same flow: thinking, imagining, testing, changing his mind, and being creative while Codex builds the next concrete version right beside him.

The daily interruptions and noise matter less when the system can hold more threads at once. Codex can juggle the backlog, the page, the repo, the copy, the next action, and the verification loop without forcing the idea to restart from zero every time.

"Is it possible to have a 10x increase in efficiency? I already have that now."

The feeling is a little wild: thrilled, energized, and, for the first time, almost like having a productive vice. The work has flipped. Ideas no longer have to wait politely in line. They can be shaped, shipped, improved, and turned into momentum.

Best practical recommendation so far: make Codex work with GitHub, because you are going to ship a lot.
